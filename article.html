Panic Attacks vs Anxiety

Zazz Reinhart

The big argument going around is the difference between an Anxiety Attack and a Panic Attack. Most people state that they are the same, but that it most certainly not true.

Panic Attacks are feelings of terror that strike without warning. Hell, you can even have one in your sleep! A panic attack almost feels like a heart attack, where the indivdual feels that they are going to die.The most common signs of a panic attack are as follows:

  • Racing heart
  • Feeling weak or having dizzy spells
  • Sense of terror
  • Breathing difficulties
  • Feeling sweaty or having chills

In contrast, anxiety is a separate experience from that of a panic attack. Anxiety, by definition, is an excessive, persisting worry over an imminent event. This can apply to anything, from something serious as death to something mundane as being late to an appointment. A common person with anxiety often shows these symptoms:

  • Stress
  • Irritability
  • Fatigue/Restlessness
  • Difficulty speaking
  • Depersonalization
  • Concentration Problems
  • Nervousness

These symptoms are tame compared to Panic Attacks, but they can be the cause. It's always best to go to your local doctor or therapist and discuss how you're feeling. You'll never know the day where you might experience a panic attack, and trust me, you never ever want to.
